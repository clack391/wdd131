Mountain Trails Site Plan

Site Name

Mountain Trails

I chose "Mountain Trails" because it clearly communicates the focus on mountain trail exploration while being memorable and easy to pronounce. The alliteration makes it catchy, and it suggests the idea of journeying to mountain summits and exploring various trail options.

Optional domain availability: mountaintrails.com

Site Purpose

Peak Paths serves as a comprehensive resource for hiking enthusiasts looking to explore mountain trails. The site provides detailed information about trail difficulty, length, elevation gain, seasonal accessibility, and points of interest. Users can find curated trail recommendations based on experience level, along with safety tips, required gear checklists, and weather advisories. The site aims to make mountain hiking more accessible by providing clear, reliable information to help hikers prepare for their adventures.

Scenarios

  • As a beginner hiker with a family, what mountain trails would be safe and enjoyable for children aged 8-12?
  • What essential equipment should I pack for a day-long intermediate mountain trail in early spring?
  • How can I find mountain trails with the best scenic views that are accessible during autumn months?

The color schema draws inspiration from the natural environment of mountain trails:

  • Primary Green (#2b6d4a): Used for headings, navigation elements, and footer. This deep forest green represents the lush vegetation and evergreen trees found on mountain trails.
  • Secondary Green (#4e8d71): Used for secondary elements, subheadings, and hover states. This lighter green provides contrast while maintaining the natural theme.
  • Accent Orange (#f9a826): Used for buttons, call-to-action elements, and highlights. This vibrant orange represents sunlight filtering through trees and draws attention to important elements.
  • Text Dark Gray (#333333): Used for body text and content. This dark gray ensures readability while being less harsh than pure black.

Typography selection:

  • Montserrat: Used for all headings (h1, h2, h3). This bold, sturdy font reflects the rugged nature of mountain landscapes while maintaining excellent readability.
  • Open Sans: Used for body text, descriptions, and general content. This clean, modern sans-serif font ensures easy reading across devices and sizes.
  • Cabin Sketch: Used sparingly for special emphasis, callouts, or decorative elements. This playful font adds a touch of adventure to selected content without compromising overall professionalism.
